The 16 ties

Claude’s logic on the close calls ▾
  • The approach: in this 48-team format, twelve group winners mostly drew third-placed teams — real mismatches, not coin flips. So this is a deliberate trust-the-form bracket; the judgment is saved for the few ties between evenly-matched sides.
  • South Africa–Canada → Canada. Near coin flip. Co-host pedigree (Davies, Jonathan David) edges it, even with Ismael Koné out injured.
  • Brazil–Japan → Brazil. Vinícius has 4 goals and the Seleção are flowing under Ancelotti. Japan are dangerous, but a level below.
  • Netherlands–Morocco → Netherlands. The scariest “favourite” draw on the board — Morocco were 2022 semi-finalists and held Brazil. Dutch form (5-1 over Sweden) just tips it.
  • Ivory Coast–Norway → Norway. Genuine 50/50; the tactical read slightly favours Ivory Coast’s press, but Haaland’s one-game ceiling and a full-strength XI returning win out.
  • Belgium–Senegal → Belgium. Aging and underwhelming in the group, but De Bruyne–Lukaku–Doku quality, and Senegal lost both their real tests (France, Norway).
  • Portugal–Croatia → Portugal. The contrarian one. Croatia shipped 4 to England and lean on a 40-year-old Modrić; Portugal’s Vitinha–João Neves–Bruno midfield is the stronger engine even with Ronaldo clogging the attack.
  • Australia–Egypt → Egypt. Salah’s ceiling plus a stronger group (5 points, drew Belgium).
